Free Convection

1.   External Flow

1.1.   Parameters

A geometry configuration among the following can be selected:

  • vertical plane
  • inclined plane top
  • inclined plane bottom
  • horizontal plane top
  • horizontal plane bottom
  • vertical cylinder
  • horizontal cylinder
  • sphere
  • finned pipe
  • convection coefficient

where the convection coefficient option allows for calculating the heat flow rate directly from a user input for the convection coefficient.

The thermal inputs include the surface temperature of the solid body (\(T_{s}\)) and the fluid temperature far from the surface (\(T_{\infty}\)). The computation can be done either at \(T_{s}\) or the mean temperature \(T_{*}=\frac{1}{2}\left(T_{s}+T_{\infty}\right)\) and is also customizable by an input for the heat exchange gain factor.

The convection coefficient \(\alpha\) is calculated from the formula:

\begin{equation*} \alpha=\frac{Nu\cdot\lambda}{L} \end{equation*}

where \(Nu\) is the Nusselt number, \(\lambda\) is the thermal conductivity, and \(L\) is the characteristic length. The latter is determined for each specific geometry configuration as follows:

  • \(h\) for vertical plane, inclined plane and vertical cylinder
  • \(d\) for horizontal cylinder and sphere
  • \(\frac{d}{4}\) for a horizontal plane with a circular shape
  • \(\frac{w\cdot l}{2\left(w+l\right)}\) for a horizontal plane with a rectangular shape
  • \(d+h_{f}\) for finned pipe

The heat flow rate \(\dot{Q}\) is then derived from the formula:

\begin{equation*} \dot{Q}=\alpha\cdot A\cdot\Delta T \end{equation*}

where \(A\) is the surface area of the solid body and \(\Delta T\) is the temperature difference.

The Nusselt number \(Nu\) is determined for each configuration by a specific \(Nu=f\left(Ra,Pr\right)\) correlation, where \(Ra\) is the Rayleigh number and \(Pr\) is the Prandtl number:

1.1.1.   Vertical plane

\begin{equation*} Nu=\left\{ 0.825+0.387\left[Ra\cdot f_{1}\left(Pr\right)\right]^{\frac{1}{6}}\right\} ^{2} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} f_{1}\left(Pr\right)=\left[1+\left(\frac{0.492}{Pr}\right)^{\frac{9}{16}}\right]^{-\frac{16}{9}} \end{equation*}

1.1.2.   Vertical cylinder

\begin{equation*} Nu=Nu_{plate}+0.97\frac{h}{d} \end{equation*}

where \(Nu_{plate}\) is the Nusselt number for a vertical plate with height \(h\)

1.1.3.   Inclined plane

Let \(\alpha\) be the angle of inclination to the vertical. The are two cases:

1.1.3.1.   The surface transmits heat downward or absorbs heat from above

In the equation for vertical plane \(Ra_{\alpha}=Ra\cdot \cos (\alpha)\) is substituted for \(Ra\).

1.1.3.2.   The surface transmits heat upward or absorbs heat from below

\begin{equation*} Nu=0.56\left(Ra_{c}\cdot \cos (\alpha)\right)^{\frac{1}{4}}+0.13\left(Ra^{\frac{1}{3}}-Ra_{c}^{\frac{1}{3}}\right) \end{equation*}

where \(Ra_{c}\) is derived from

\begin{equation*} Ra_{c}=10^{\left(8.9-0.00178\cdot\alpha^{1.82}\right)} \end{equation*}

1.1.4.   Horizontal plane

There are two cases:

1.1.4.1.   Heat is emitted at the upper surface

If

\begin{equation*} Ra\cdot f_{1}\left(Pr\right)<7\cdot10^{4} \end{equation*}

then

\begin{equation*} Nu=0.766\left[Ra\cdot f_{1}\left(Pr\right)\right]^{\frac{1}{5}} \end{equation*}

Otherwise

\begin{equation*} Nu=0.15\left[Ra\cdot f_{1}\left(Pr\right)\right]^{\frac{1}{3}} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} f_{1}\left(Pr\right)=\left[1+\left(\frac{0.322}{Pr}\right)^{\frac{11}{20}}\right]^{-\frac{20}{11}} \end{equation*}

1.1.4.2.   Heat is emitted at the lower surface

\begin{equation*} Nu=0.6\left[Ra\cdot f_{1}(Pr)\right]^{\frac{1}{5}} \end{equation*}

on condition that

\begin{equation*} 10^{3}<Ra\cdot f_{1}\left(Pr\right)<10^{10} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} f_{1}\left(Pr\right)=\left[1+\left(\frac{0.492}{Pr}\right)^{\frac{9}{16}}\right]^{-\frac{16}{9}} \end{equation*}

1.1.5.   Horizontal cylinder

\begin{equation*} Nu=\left\{ 0.60+0.387\left[Ra\cdot f_{1}\left(Pr\right)\right]^{\frac{1}{6}}\right\} ^{2} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} f_{1}\left(Pr\right)=\left[1+\left(\frac{0.559}{Pr}\right)^{\frac{9}{16}}\right]^{-\frac{16}{9}} \end{equation*}

1.1.6.   Sphere

\begin{equation*} Nu=0.56\left[\left(\frac{Pr}{0.846+Pr}\right)Ra\right]^{\frac{1}{4}}+2 \end{equation*}

1.1.7.   Finned pipe

\begin{equation*} Nu=0.24\left(Ra\frac{b}{d}\right)^{\frac{1}{3}} \end{equation*}

where \(b\) is the fin spacing and \(d\) is the diameter of the core pipe. The area used to calculate the heat exchange rate is the total surface area of the core pipe and fins.
